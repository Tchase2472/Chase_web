What Is Up Today?

Author: Todd Chase

 Category:

Personal Account

.....And so it begins.


The job search has entered its' first month. There have been no offers. There has been a lot of support though.

Its more impotant than anyone thinks, actually.

I don't think I would be able to continue without it.

Ok, ok, I get it, it's only been a month.


Well,.........not quite. The journey to the holy coding space has actually been going on now for not quite 4 years.

It has been a much longer road than I would have ever suspected. And it has been fraught with troubles.

I'd like to look a bit deeper at the road I have taken to get here.


When I started learning web development I had absolutely no previous knowledge of programming.

I worked in an office. I could create a Word document and maybe a nice spreadsheet. That's about it.

I was thought of as a "computer guy" because I could make the printer work sometimes.

It's laughable to think I was ever considered anything like a "computer guy" knowing what I know now.

Anyway, "things" happened and I found myself looking for a new career. My wife suggested web development.

This was interesting. I thought, "Yeah, I can do that. Easy." Yeah, OK, when you've stopped laughing I will continue.


And so it began. I had no idea where to even start. I hopped on the computer and did some research. It seems that the place to start

was to learn something called "javascript". Ok, I can do that. Won't take a minute......

This was the first stop on the road to the holy coding space. And it was my first error. Now, don't get me wrong,

you do need to know javascript. You need HTML and CSS as well, of course.

No, the error was diving head first into the language without bothering to learn all the things that go in, on, over, and around the language.

There is a lot of stuff that is not, strictly speaking, javascript, but is vital to know about javascript.


A lot of people are going to disagree with me here. I do understand, and I agree, you have to start somewhere, don't you? Yes, you do.

My point here is that there is a Level 0 before there is a Level 1.


Level 0, you say? What the hell is Level 0? Level 0 is orientation. We often think of our computers to consist of "environments" or "spaces".

It is vital that a new student be introduced the what a thing is and where the thing is in a certain space and what is the relationship between these different spaces and the objects that inhabit them.

This is what I mean by the things in, on, over, and around the language.

You may take this to mean teaching the newbie about code editors and other such peripheral tools. No, I mean introducing the new guy to the simple basics.

Things like what a computer is. But not just in terms of the machine itself, but rather how the language interacts with that machine, and why.

There is no substitute for actual understanding as opposed to learning "just enough to get a job". It all starts with Level 0.

Now, I understand, there is A LOT to know at this level. But I believe it is time well spent. Once you get past that hurdle, the rest will still be tough, but not impossible.


It is important to remember that this is NOT the way I did it. I jumped in head first into the shallow end of the pool. I spent years

trying to figure all this stuff out. I am 100% not an expert at all.

But I have learned one painful lesson, there are no shorcuts.

There is, however, a better way than jumping around from one thing to another in a desparate frenzy trying to understand something.

It's learning the bare bone basics.


I think it is fair to take everything I have said with some grains of salt. After all, I haven't found my next job yet either.

But I have been walking this road for some time now.

And I have walked up many blind alleys. And I have fallen for many "fads". In fact, I think I have made every mistake you can make.

All I ask is that, if you're having trouble or feel like you should give up, don't.

Look for one thing you really understand, then do that thing. Then take the next thing you really understand, and so on.

If you get stuck, settle in and learn the basics of the thing. Time is a secondary concern to understanding.

Anyway, that's the story up to now. I'll check in later. I wish you the best of luck, and maybe someday we will meet at the holy coding space at last.
